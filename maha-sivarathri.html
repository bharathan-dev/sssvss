About Maha Sivarathri

Maha Sivarathri — the great night of Lord Shiva — is one of the most significant nights in the Hindu calendar. SSSVSS marks this occasion with Ekadasa Rudra Abhiseka (sacred bathing of the Shiva Lingam with chanting of Rudram) performed four times, once in each of the four Kalas (time periods) of the night.

Sacred Night Schedule

Four Kalas

Ekadasa Rudra Abhiseka is performed at four specific time periods through the night. Each Kala consists of Ekadasa Rudram chanting followed by Abhishekam.

Kala 1
Pratham Kala
6:00 PM – 9:00 PM · Ekadasa Rudram + Abhishekam
Kala 2
Dwitiya Kala
9:00 PM – 12:00 AM · Ekadasa Rudram + Abhishekam
Kala 3
Tritiya Kala
12:00 AM – 3:00 AM · Ekadasa Rudram + Abhishekam
Kala 4
Chaturtha Kala
3:00 AM – 6:00 AM · Ekadasa Rudram + Abhishekam

FAQ

Yes — all four Kalas are open to devotees. You are welcome to attend any or all of the four sessions. Many devotees stay through the night for the full blessing.

Please bring a shawl or light jacket as it can be cool through the night. Traditional attire is recommended. Prasadam is served after each Kala.
