About Ekadasa Rudram

Ekadasa Rudram involves the collective recitation of the sacred Sri Rudram — comprising Namakam and Chamakam — eleven times by a group of eleven chanters. The vibrations of this powerful chant are believed to purify the environment and invoke divine blessings.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Ekadasa Rudram involves the chanting of Sri Rudram — which comprises Namakam and Chamakam from the Krishna Yajurveda — eleven times by eleven devoted chanters simultaneously. The word "Ekadasa" means eleven in Sanskrit.

Bhagawan Sri Sathya Sai Baba has spoken extensively on the power of Rudram, describing how its vibrations purify the atmosphere, invoke divine grace, and align the chanter with Universal Consciousness. SSSVSS holds this as its founding and most sacred practice.

Ekadasa Rudram is held twice a month, on the day of Nakshatra Arudhra — the birth star of Bhagawan Sri Sathya Sai Baba. The exact date varies each month based on the Hindu lunar calendar.

Please check the calendar above or subscribe to the Google Calendar to receive automatic reminders.

Yes, absolutely. All devotees are welcome to attend and participate in any way they are comfortable — whether chanting, listening, or simply being present in the sacred atmosphere.

If you wish to learn the chanting, SSSVSS conducts regular Rudram classes. Please see the Vedic Classes section for the current schedule and registration details.

Hosting Ekadasa Rudram at your home is considered a great blessing and a form of seva. To offer your home:

  • Submit a hosting request using the form in the Hosting section above
  • The initiative lead will confirm the date and add your address to the Google Calendar event
  • Your full address is only shared with registered participants — it is not displayed publicly on this website

For preparation details, please download the Host Preparation Checklist from the Purchase List section.

A standard session requires exactly 11 chanters. Total attendance, including family members and other devotees, typically ranges from 15 to 30 people. Please ensure your home can comfortably accommodate this number in a prayer space.

Sessions typically begin at 8:00 AM and last approximately 3 hours, including the chanting, Abhishekam (where applicable), Mangala Arati, and prasadam.

Participants are welcome to arrive 15 minutes early to help with setup and preparation.

Please reach out to the initiative lead listed at the top of this page. For general SSSVSS enquiries, you may also use the contact form on the main website or reach us via our WhatsApp group.
