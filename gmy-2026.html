What is it

About Gayatri Maha Yagna

"The Gayatri Mantra is the embodiment of all the Vedas. Whoever meditates on it and repeats it will be protected by the Gayatri." — Bhagawan Sri Sathya Sai Baba

The Gayatri Maha Yagna is an annual large-scale fire sacrifice (Homa) and collective chanting of the Gayatri Mantra, drawing devotees from across Singapore and beyond. It is one of the most auspicious events in the SSSVSS calendar.

The three-day event includes Sankalpa, Homa, Purnahuti, and concludes with Mangala Arati and Annadanam for all participants. Ritwiks perform the sacred fire ritual while devotees participate through Japa, prayers, and service.

Day 1 · 8 May
Sankalpa & Commencement
Opening prayers, Sankalpa, and commencement of Gayatri Homa. Morning session begins 8:00 AM.
Day 2 · 9 May
Maha Homa
Full-day Homa with continuous Gayatri Japa. Ritwiks and chanters in rotation. Open to all devotees.
Day 3 · 10 May
Purnahuti & Mangala Arati
Concluding Homa, Purnahuti, Mangala Arati, and Annadanam (blessed meal) for all present.
Throughout
Japa & Prayers
Devotees contribute through individual and collective Gayatri Japa — counts submitted online and tallied in real time.
Prepare Yourself

Japa Sankalpa & Daily Sadhana

In the weeks leading up to GMY, devotees are invited to take a Sankalpa — a sacred resolve — to chant the Gayatri Mantra daily and contribute their count to the collective Japa offering.

🙏
Take the Japa Sankalpa
Commit to a daily count of Gayatri Mantra repetitions from now until the Yagna. Your sankalpa becomes part of the collective offering to Bhagawan.
  • Choose your daily Japa count — 108, 216, or 1008
  • Chant at a fixed time each day, preferably at dawn
  • Submit your count weekly using the Japa form below
  • All counts are tallied and offered at Purnahuti

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Gayatri Maha Yagna (GMY) is a large-scale fire sacrifice (Homa) dedicated to the Gayatri Mantra — the most sacred of all Vedic mantras. The event brings together trained Ritwiks to perform the Homa over three days while devotees participate through collective Japa, prayers, and service. SSSVSS has been conducting GMY annually, and the 2026 edition is especially significant as part of Bhagawan Sri Sathya Sai Baba's Centennial year.

The Japa Sankalpa is a sacred resolve to chant the Gayatri Mantra a fixed number of times daily in the weeks leading up to GMY. Your count becomes part of a collective offering made during Purnahuti on the final day.

To participate: choose your daily target (108, 216, or 1008 repetitions), chant at a consistent time each day, and submit your weekly count using the form on this page. All counts are tallied and displayed in the live total above.

Ritwiks are devotees who are proficient in the Gayatri Mantra and the Homa procedure. Prior experience in SSSVSS Vedic classes or demonstrated chanting ability is preferred. If you wish to participate as a Ritwik, please register using the link above — the GMY team will contact you for a brief assessment.

GMY 2026 will be held at Holy Tree Sri Subramaniar Temple, Sengkang, Singapore. The event is open to all devotees — no registration is required to attend as an observer or participant. Attendees are welcome on any or all three days and will receive prasadam.

Participants submit their Japa count through the Google Form on this page. Submissions are automatically recorded in a Google Sheet. The initiative lead reviews and approves entries, and the running total is updated regularly (displayed live on this page). The final cumulative total is announced and offered during Purnahuti on Day 3.

Devotees are encouraged to wear traditional attire — saree or salwar for ladies, dhoti or veshti for gents. Ritwiks must wear dhoti. Please arrive with a clean, sattvic mindset and avoid non-vegetarian food on the day of attendance.

Bring your own japa mala if you intend to chant during the event. Prasadam will be provided on all three days.
