Prepare Yourself

Japa Sankalpa & Daily Sadhana

In the days preceding SGMY, devotees take a Sankalpa — a sacred resolve — to chant the Gayatri Mantra daily and contribute to the collective Japa offering at Purnahuti. There are three Sankalpa opportunities.

🙏
Physical Sankalpa
An in-person Sankalpa gathering where devotees make their resolve together — the most powerful form of collective intention.
Around Chinese New Year period (date announced via WhatsApp)
💻
Digital Sankalpas (×2)
Two online Sankalpa sessions within 45 days of SGMY, open to all devotees regardless of location.
Digital Sankalpa 1 — within 45 days of 8 May 2026
Digital Sankalpa 2 — within 45 days of 8 May 2026
90-Day Daily Prayers Sadhana Alongside Japa Sankalpa, devotees join the daily 6:30–7:00 AM digital morning prayers for 90 days prior to SGMY. See the 90-Day Prayers page for the full programme and how to join.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

The Sri Gayatri Maha Yagna is an annual large-scale fire sacrifice (Homa) and collective chanting of the Gayatri Mantra, conducted by SSSVSS. The 2026 edition is a special centennial offering in conjunction with Bhagawan Sri Sathya Sai Baba's Araadhana Mahotsavam.

The 2026 event is at Field 2, 24 Geylang East Avenue 2, Singapore 389752 — opposite Sri Sivan Temple. This is an open field venue. Please check the Google Maps link in the Venue section for directions.

On Saturday, 9th May (Day 2), a Blood Donation Drive is conducted from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM alongside the religious programme. This is SSSVSS's act of community seva — donating blood is considered one of the highest forms of selfless service. All eligible devotees are encouraged to donate.

There are two tracks: (1) Public submission via the Google Form on this page — open to all devotees, contributions are tallied and displayed as a cumulative total. (2) Registered database users — devotees registered by admin in the GMY2026 database can submit counts with full tracking, duplicate validation, and per-user / block-level reporting. Contact admin to be added as a registered user.

Traditional attire is encouraged — saree or salwar for ladies, dhoti or veshti for gents. Ritwiks must wear dhoti. Bring a japa mala if you intend to chant. Please observe a vegetarian diet on days of attendance. Prasadam is provided at all sessions.
